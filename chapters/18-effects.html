Effects (FX1 & FX2)

The full catalogue of Octatrack effects, plus the famous FX1 reverb hack — a workaround that turns FX1 into a reverb slot despite its limited official choices.

Effect slots

Each track has FX1 and FX2 slots. FX1 runs first; FX2 runs after. The choice of which effect lives in each slot is set on the FX page's Setup screen (FUNCTION + EFFECT 1 or EFFECT 2).

Note that FX1 and FX2 have different — but partly overlapping — catalogs. Filter, EQ, and Lo-Fi appear on both. Reverb and delay are FX2-only.

The FX1 reverb hack

Officially, reverb is FX2-only. But because FX1 has a Spatializer that includes a short ambience component, and because Lo-Fi can be pushed into a degraded near-reverb texture, it's possible to get reverb-like behaviour from FX1 — freeing FX2 for a separate use.

The cleaner method, however: use a Neighbor machine on the next track. Track 1 has FX1 + FX2 (e.g. filter + delay). Track 2 is a Neighbor reading T1's output, with FX1 = filter and FX2 = reverb. Now you have effectively four effects on T1's audio path. The "reverb hack" is mostly historical — the Neighbor approach is more flexible and is documented in chapter 14.

FX setup conventions

Common combinations:

  • Filter + Reverb — most versatile, classic sample chain
  • Compressor + Delay — compressed source into delay, useful for vocals or punchy melodics
  • Lo-Fi + Reverb — degraded character into space
  • EQ + Compressor (on a Master machine) — master-bus glue
  • Phaser + Delay — psychedelic textures, swirl + space

P-locking FX parameters

Every FX parameter can be p-locked. This is where the OT's effects become sequenceable: a delay's feedback can rise and fall across a pattern, a reverb's mix can fade in for two bars, a filter's cutoff can sweep on every step.

Combined with LFO modulation (chapter 17) on the same parameters, you can stack rhythmic FX changes (p-locks) over continuous modulation (LFOs) for performative complexity.

Tip · the wet/dry parameter (F)

Most effects' Mix parameter is on encoder F. P-locking F to 0 on certain steps disables the effect for those trigs only — useful for "this kick has reverb, this kick doesn't" effects without muting the FX globally.

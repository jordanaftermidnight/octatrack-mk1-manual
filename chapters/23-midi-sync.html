MIDI sync & clocking

Configuring the Octatrack as MIDI master, slave, or in daisy-chained setups with multiple devices.

Sync modes

PROJECT → SYNC SOURCE has three options:

  • INT — internal clock. The OT's tempo is master.
  • MIDI — slave to incoming MIDI clock on the IN port.
  • USB — slave to USB MIDI (rare; requires host software supporting USB MIDI clock).

The OT as master

Default mode. The OT generates clock and sends it on the OUT port. Connected slaves (synths, drum machines, other Elektron units) follow the OT's tempo and transport.

To send clock: PROJECT → MIDI → SYNC → CLOCK SEND = ON. The OT transmits 24 PPQ MIDI clock continuously when running.

The OT also sends MIDI Start, Stop, and Continue messages aligned with its transport. Most slaves respect these.

The OT as slave

Set SYNC SOURCE = MIDI. The OT's transport now follows incoming MIDI clock. Press PLAY on the master device; the OT starts. Stop on master; OT stops.

The OT's internal tempo display shows the received tempo. Tempo changes on the master propagate immediately.

Caveat: when slaved, the OT's tempo knob is disabled. To fine-tune tempo, do it on the master.

Daisy-chaining

A daisy chain uses MIDI THRU to forward incoming clock from one device to the next. Master → slave 1 (IN, THRU) → slave 2 (IN, THRU) → ...

The OT's THRU port forwards exactly what arrives at IN — no re-clocking, no re-buffering. So long as the OT is in slave mode, its THRU is functionally a passthrough.

Latency considerations

MIDI clock has finite resolution — 24 PPQ at MIDI's standard baud introduces a jitter floor of ~1 ms. For most performance applications this is inaudible. For tight synchronisation (parallel drum machines hitting the same beat) you may want TurboMIDI between two Elektron devices, which reduces clock jitter.

On non-Elektron daisy chains, expect a few milliseconds of cumulative delay across multiple THRU stages. Splitter boxes (re-clocking distribution amplifiers) help on long chains.

Project-wide MIDI settings

Beyond sync source, several MIDI settings live at the project level (PROJECT → MIDI):

  • CLOCK SEND — transmit clock
  • CLOCK RECV — receive clock
  • TRANSPORT SEND — transmit Start/Stop/Continue
  • TRANSPORT RECV — receive transport messages
  • PROGRAM CHANGE SEND/RECV — pattern changes as MIDI Program Change
  • CC SEND — send CC for OT parameters (rare; for external monitoring or recording)

Using OT pattern changes as PC

With PROGRAM CHANGE SEND = ON, switching patterns on the OT sends a MIDI PC message corresponding to the pattern number. Slaves that listen for PC can switch their own patterns in sync.

Practical use: OT pattern A1 sends PC 0; the connected Digitone switches to its pattern 0. OT to A2 sends PC 1; Digitone switches to 1. With careful pattern numbering, both devices can step through aligned arrangements.

Note · USB does not carry audio or MIDI clock

The Octatrack's USB port is for SysEx (firmware updates, project backup) and disk-mode access only. There is no USB MIDI, no USB audio, no Overbridge — even though many of Elektron's other products support these. The OT MK1 was developed before USB MIDI/audio became standard on Elektron's lineup. Don't expect it.

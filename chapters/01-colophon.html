A note on this manual

This is an unofficial, community-oriented rework of the Elektron Octatrack MK1 manual. It exists because the official documentation, while accurate, has long been considered one of the steeper hurdles a new owner faces. The Octatrack is one of the deepest sampler-sequencers ever built — and the gap between what it can do and what most owners ever discover is enormous.

This rework consolidates Elektron's official manual (OS 1.40A, December 2020) and the OS 1.40C release notes (January 2024 — the firmware shipping on the final production units) with material drawn from the wider Octatrack community: the Rabid Elephant Master Reference, Merlin's Guide, NoiseTheorem's cheat sheet, and the long-running Elektronauts forum threads where most undocumented behaviour was first surfaced. Source attributions appear at the end of the relevant section.

Nothing in this manual is endorsed by Elektron Music Machines MAV AB. "Octatrack", "Elektron", and related marks belong to their respective holders. This document reproduces no copyrighted text from the official manual; all explanations are written fresh, and short factual quotations (where they appear) are clearly marked.

How to read this manual

The manual is structured in twelve parts, moving from orientation (Parts I–II) through conceptual scaffolding (Part III), then operation (Parts IV–IX), into performance and recipes (Parts X–XI), and finishing with a complete reference (Part XII).

A complete beginner should read Parts I–IV linearly. An owner who has used the machine for some time can skip to Part V (machines), Part VIII (scenes and the crossfader), or Part XI (recipes). The reference appendices, especially the complete button-combo encyclopedia, are designed to be browsed independently and printed if useful.

Conventions used

Front-panel keys
Shown as FUNCTION, REC, PLAY. Combinations: FUNCTION+REC means hold the first key while pressing the second.
Trig keys
The 16 step buttons are shown as TRIG 1…TRIG 16 when context-specific, otherwise TRIG.
Parameters
Octatrack parameter names appear in monospace small caps, e.g. PTCH, RLEN, XVOL, mirroring the device screen.
Menu paths
Written as PROJECT → SYSTEM → CARD TOOLS → FORMAT.
Callouts
Orange callouts mark tips the official manual omits. Black callouts mark hidden features. Red callouts mark warnings. Blue callouts mark notes on quirks and gotchas.

Sources & acknowledgements

The structural authority for this manual is the official Elektron Octatrack User Manual (OS 1.40A, December 2020) and the OS 1.40C release notes (January 2024). Beyond the official sources, the following community works deserve direct credit:

  • The Rabid Elephant Master Reference — the most thorough community document of Octatrack workflow lore, hidden behaviour, and edge-case troubleshooting.
  • Merlin's Guide ("Some Thoughts on Elektron's Octatrack") — for the clearest existing conceptual framing of parts, scenes, and the bank/pattern hierarchy.
  • NoiseTheorem's cheat sheet — for compact key-combo coverage.
  • The Elektronauts forum, especially the long-running threads on conditional trigs, FX1 reverb, Pickup machine behaviour, the crossfader transition, and the early-startup test mode.
  • Cuckoo, Dataline (Cenk Sayinli), Mark Mosher, Joseph Miller (Sounds Like Joe), and Tarekith — for the video-tutorial and written workflow material from which several recipes in Part XI are derived.

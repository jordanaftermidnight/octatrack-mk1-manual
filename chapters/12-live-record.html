Chapter 12

Live record & preview

Real-time pattern entry and the audition workflow. Two complementary modes: live record captures performance into the sequencer; preview auditions trigs without committing them.

Live Record

Live Record lets you record trigs and parameter changes while the sequencer plays. Two flavours:

Unquantised Live Record

Activate: hold REC + PLAY. The OT enters Live Record mode (REC LED flashing). Any trig key you press is recorded at the exact moment it occurs — including its microtiming offset relative to the nearest step.

Use case: live, expressive entry where exact timing matters. Pressing trigs slightly behind the beat creates lay-back; ahead of the beat creates push. Useful for finger-drumming and chromatic melodic input.

Quantised Live Record

Activate: hold FUNCTION + REC + PLAY. Any trig you press is snapped to the nearest step. Microtiming is zeroed.

Use case: tighter, more rigid recording — useful when capturing chromatic input and you want clean step-aligned trigs.

What gets recorded

  • Trig presses (TRACKS mode) — added as note trigs at their timing
  • Trig presses (CHROMATIC mode) — recorded as note trigs with pitch p-locks
  • Trig presses (SLICES mode) — recorded with slice-number p-locks
  • Knob movements — recorded as p-locks on the step the playhead passes through
  • Sample slot changes (SLOTS mode) — recorded as slot p-locks

Erasing on the fly

Hold FUNCTION + NO while the sequencer plays in Live Record. Any step the playhead passes through is erased — both the trig and any p-locks on it. Release to stop erasing.

This is the OT's "moving rubber" — useful for quickly clearing a section of the pattern without exiting Live Record.

Preview

Preview lets you hear what a step would sound like (with its p-locks) without committing to it.

Two preview destinations:

  • YES + TRIG — preview to MAIN bus
  • CUE + TRIG — preview to CUE bus (headphone pre-listen)

Both forms play the trig's sample with the trig's parameter locks applied. Use case: while building a pattern, audition each step's locked values before deciding whether to keep them.

Pattern length and per-track length

Pattern length is configured per pattern via FUNCTION+SCALE SETUP. The OT supports patterns from 1 to 64 steps.

Per-track length: each track can have its own pattern length within the master pattern. This creates polymeter — track 1 loops at 16 steps while track 2 loops at 13 steps, drifting in and out of phase.

Master length sets the overall pattern duration; track lengths are capped at master. When a track's length is shorter than master, the track repeats internally until master cycles.

Time scale

The time scale (1/4, 1/8, 1/16, 1/32) determines how fast the sequencer runs through its steps relative to the project tempo. Default 1/16 — each step is a sixteenth note. Change to 1/32 for fast hi-hat or trap-style sequences; 1/8 for half-time feels.

Time scale is per-track. A 1/32 hi-hat track on a 1/16 master pattern fires twice as fast — but loops twice within the same master cycle, so the timing aligns musically.

The 1:4 / 2:4 / 3:4 / 4:4 LEDs

The four LEDs above the Scale Setup button (callout 26 in chapter 4) flash on each beat of the master pattern, showing your position within the bar. They're a visual metronome — useful for live performance when you can't take your eyes off the panel.

Recording knobs and parameter changes

In Live Record, knob movements are recorded as parameter locks on every step the playhead passes through during the move. Move a filter knob through one cycle of the pattern, and you've drawn an automation curve into the sequencer.

The recorded automation can be edited per-step afterwards — hold a trig to see the locked value, turn the knob to adjust. Combined with Live Record's trig entry, this is how complex evolving patterns get built on the OT in minutes rather than hours.

Tip · stop with sequencer playing

To exit Live Record without stopping the sequencer, press REC alone. This disarms recording but keeps playback running. Useful for iterative composition: enter Live Record, add some trigs, exit to listen, re-enter to add more.
